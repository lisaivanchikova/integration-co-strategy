03 · The Replication Unit

The unit that scales is the vertical-specific deployment playbook applied to each acquired OpCo — not the engineer, the customer, or the dollar.

Each business model has a different replication unit with a different bottleneck and ceiling. The HoldCo's primary replication unit is the acquired company; the engine that compresses time-to-uplift on each new acquisition is the vertical-specific playbook. Long Lake's deployment time compressed from over a year on early deals to days on recent acquisitions in the same vertical — the playbook itself became the compounding asset.

The playbook in six components

The strategic question is which unit scales the operating company. The answer is the playbook — a pre-built vertical-specific package the team plugs into each engagement. The playbook is what makes a 5-day customizer beat a 30-day bespoke build. Six components:

01 · Vertical thesisWhy this vertical's SMEs deserve AI integration, what AI can actually move (labor cost, error rate, ticket volume).
02 · Baseline kitPre-built measurement infrastructure for L1 diagnostic — KPIs, data taps, comparison benchmarks.
03 · Reference architectureStack of agents / workflows / integrations validated in prior engagements — 70% reusable, 30% customized.
04 · Pricing templateL1 fee / L2 gainshare cap / L3 warrant terms calibrated for vertical-specific EBITDA and exit-multiple ranges.
05 · Operations runbook30/60/90 day deployment checklist, escalation paths, SLA monitoring.
06 · Acquisition due-diligence packPre-completed financial / tech-debt / customer-concentration audit format for L3 conversion candidates.

Leverage hierarchy: Engineer (linear) < Customer-unit (compounding) < Playbook (step-function) ≈ Acquired-Co (capital-bound). The HoldCo stacks playbook + acquired-Co — the playbook compresses each new acquisition's integration time toward zero, while acquired-Co is the primary unit on which capital is deployed.

04 · The Single Motion

One motion — vertical roll-ups gated by AI productivity validation on an operator-partner before any acquisition capital is committed.

The HoldCo runs one motion: select a vertical, find an operator-partner inside it, run a paid pilot where the AI Engineering arm validates ≥1.5× productivity uplift inside the operator's live workflow, then commit acquisition capital and roll up the vertical with the validated AI stack deployed from day one. The acquired operator becomes the platform; subsequent SMBs are tucked in under the same playbook. AI capability never leaves the portfolio — it is not sold externally and not licensed to any third party.

What we do

Select Hell-Yes / Strong verticals from the V×C×A×B×M scoring map (1,012 NAICS 6-digit codes). Find an industry-insider operator in the selected vertical. Run a paid pilot inside their workflow. Validate ≥1.5× productivity. Acquire the operator's company. Roll up the vertical.

What we do not do

Sell AI services to third-party SMEs. License AI products to operators outside our portfolio. Run a services arm with external customer revenue. These actions trigger services-multiple compression at exit and create Section 206 conflicts with future portfolio acquisitions.

Financing the L2 pilot fee — how operator-partners without $200K cash run pilots

The operator-partner often does not have $200K of discretionary cash for a pilot. We solve it by financing the pilot fee against the acquisition consideration we will pay if the pilot succeeds. Four paths, used in a blend:
